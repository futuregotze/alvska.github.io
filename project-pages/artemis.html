Artemis Capstone Project

Follow the phases of the Artemis Capstone Project to enhance cybersecurity resilience. This comprehensive project involves reconnaissance, vulnerability scanning, threat assessment, and risk mitigation. Each phase is carefully crafted to provide actionable insights for fortifying Artemis, Incorporated against cyber threats.

Artemis Capstone Project: Phase 1

In the initial phase of the Artemis Capstone Project, our focus is on performing thorough reconnaissance to build a comprehensive profile of Artemis, Incorporated. The primary goal is to gather publicly available information that provides insight into the organization's digital footprint, potential vulnerabilities, and overall security posture.

As cybersecurity consultants, our team will employ a strategic blend of tools and methods, ranging from advanced Google searches and WHOIS lookups to social media analysis and DNS interrogation. By harnessing these resources, we aim to understand Artemis deeply, ensuring a solid foundation for subsequent phases of the penetration test. This reconnaissance phase not only sets the stage for a successful engagement but also underscores our commitment to delivering tailored cybersecurity solutions that address Artemis' unique challenges and concerns. Let's embark on this crucial first step towards fortifying Artemis against emerging cyber threats.

Procedure:

  1. Google Search: Use advanced Google searches to find information about Artemis.
  2. WHOIS Lookup: Look up domain registration details to understand Artemis' online presence.
  3. Social Media Check: Review social media platforms for insights into Artemis' structure and potential risks.

Artemis Capstone Project: Phase 2

In the second phase of the Artemis Capstone Project, our primary focus shifts to meticulously identifying potential targets and executing scans to unveil crucial details about Artemis, Incorporated. This stage is pivotal for comprehending the organization's network architecture and vulnerabilities. Our cybersecurity consulting team will utilize a comprehensive set of tools, including Nmap, OpenVAS, Metasploit, Nikto, Lynis, Fierce, Wireshark, and Aircrack-ng. Each tool has a distinct role, contributing to the overall success of the penetration test. However, it is essential to acknowledge the challenges and potential limitations associated with each tool, as navigating through these nuances ensures that our subsequent actions align with the overarching objective of fortifying Artemis against cyber threats.

Artemis Capstone Project: Phase 3

As we delve into Phase 3 of the Artemis Capstone Project, our mission pivots towards the crucial task of identifying vulnerabilities within Artemis, Incorporated's digital landscape. In this phase, our cybersecurity team is geared to deploy an array of cutting-edge tools, including OpenVAS, Tenable Nessus, Burp Suite, Wapiti, W3af, and XSSPY. Our objective is to conduct comprehensive vulnerability scans, scrutinizing the organization's systems, applications, and network infrastructure. By meticulously identifying potential weaknesses, we aim to equip Artemis with the insights necessary to fortify its security posture effectively.

(GVM) OpenVAS:

Purpose: OpenVAS is a robust open-source vulnerability scanner designed to identify and assess security issues within networks.

Usage: Configuring OpenVAS, our team will perform a comprehensive scan of Artemis' infrastructure, highlighting vulnerabilities across various network components.

Tenable Nessus:

Purpose: Nessus is a widely-utilized vulnerability scanning tool known for identifying and assessing vulnerabilities across diverse assets.

Usage: Leveraging Nessus, our team will conduct detailed vulnerability assessments on Artemis' systems, providing in-depth insights into potential risks.

Burp Suite:

Purpose: Burp Suite is an integrated platform tailored for security testing of web applications.

Usage: Focused on web application security, Burp Suite will scrutinize Artemis' web-based systems, identifying potential vulnerabilities such as SQL injection or cross-site scripting.

Wapiti:

Purpose: Wapiti is a specialized web application vulnerability scanner designed to detect security weaknesses.

Usage: Wapiti will be employed for targeted scans of Artemis' web applications, identifying vulnerabilities exploitable by attackers.

W3af:

Purpose: W3af is a comprehensive web application attack and audit framework designed for identifying and exploiting vulnerabilities.

Usage: Our team will utilize W3af to conduct thorough audits of Artemis' web applications, uncovering potential security flaws.

XSSPY:

Purpose: XSSPY is a dedicated tool for identifying Cross-Site Scripting vulnerabilities in web applications.

Usage: Employing XSSPY, we will scrutinize Artemis' web applications for potential XSS vulnerabilities, ensuring a comprehensive assessment of security risks.

Artemis Capstone Project: Phase 4

As we transition into Phase 4 of the Artemis Capstone Project, our cybersecurity team is shifting gears to convert the results from our vulnerability scans into a comprehensive threat assessment. Following scans with tools like OpenVAS (GVM), Tenable Nessus, Burp Suite, Wapiti, W3af, and XSSPY, our focus now is to analyze identified vulnerabilities, categorize risks, and propose effective remediation strategies.

Key Summary Findings and Recommendations:

  1. Unpatched RDP Exposed to the Internet:

    Risk Level: Critical

    Recommendation: Immediate patching of Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP), implementation of Network Level Authentication (NLA), and strengthening password policies.

Artemis Inc. Vulnerability Assessment

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Artemis Inc. recently underwent a comprehensive cybersecurity risk assessment to evaluate the potential impact of internal and external threats. The assessment, conducted from January 2023, focused on identifying vulnerabilities that could compromise the organization's security. The following summary provides a high-level view of the business risks in plain English, ensuring that executives can make informed decisions.

Overall Assessment Results:

The assessment uncovered critical insights into Artemis Inc.'s cybersecurity posture, highlighting vulnerabilities that could pose significant business risks. The identified vulnerabilities fall into three main risk categories: Critical, High, and Moderate.

Critical Risks (Red): Immediate action is required to address these vulnerabilities, as they present severe threats that could lead to unauthorized access, data breaches, and potential network compromise. Failure to address critical risks promptly may result in severe business disruptions.

High Risks (Orange): These vulnerabilities pose substantial risks and require urgent attention. While not as imminent as critical risks, high-risk vulnerabilities could still lead to serious consequences if exploited. Timely remediation is essential to safeguard business operations.

Moderate Risks (Yellow): While not as severe as critical or high risks, moderate-risk vulnerabilities still present potential threats. Managing these risks is important for overall cybersecurity resilience. A proactive approach is recommended to prevent escalation into more critical issues.

Visual Representation: To enhance clarity, we have used a color-coded system

  • Red: Critical Risks
  • Orange: High Risks
  • Yellow: Moderate Risks
  • Green: No Significant Risks
Key Summary Findings and Recommendations:
  1. Unpatched RDP Exposed to the Internet:

    Risk Level: Critical

    Recommendation: Immediate patching of Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP), implementation of Network Level Authentication (NLA), and strengthening password policies.

Conclusion:

While Artemis Inc. demonstrates the ability to patch and remediate vulnerabilities, the assessment reveals potential gaps in the comprehensiveness and effectiveness of existing processes. Unmitigated vulnerabilities, if exploited, can compromise the entire corporate network. Artemis Inc. is strongly urged to explore opportunities to enhance vulnerability and patch management strategies for comprehensive risk mitigation. This executive summary serves as a strategic compass for Artemis Inc.'s cybersecurity journey.
