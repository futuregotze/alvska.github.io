Show how chords can be built using the notes from the C major scale. The most common chords in a major key are the I (tonic), IV (subdominant), and V (dominant) chords. Teach students how to construct the C major chord (I) by selecting the 1st, 3rd, and 5th scale degrees (C, E, G). Discuss the chord qualities, explaining that the I chord is major, the IV chord is major, and the V chord is major (or dominant).
